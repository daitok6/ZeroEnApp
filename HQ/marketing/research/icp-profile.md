# ICP Profile — Japanese Coach / Therapist Solopreneur

**Owner:** mktg-strategy · **Date:** 2026-04-14 · **Source segment:** [market-saturation.md](./market-saturation.md) — Segment B (score 22/25)

---

## One-line ICP

**Japan-based solo coach, therapist, or counselor, aged 32–52, currently earning ¥3M–¥12M/yr, with a launched practice but a broken or absent LP, actively trying to move from referrals-only to a predictable pipeline.**

If she fits all four lines, she is our buyer. If she fits three, she's the spillover audience.

## 2. Business stage

| Metric | Profile |

## 3. Pain points with LPs today

1. **"I spent 3 weekends on ペライチ and it still looks like everyone else's ペライチ."** Low differentiation, no brand voice.
2. **"I paid ¥200k for an HP制作 freelancer and waited 3 months. Then I needed to change my offer and they quoted another ¥80k."** Upfront-fee model breaks iteration.
3. **"My friend who's a designer said she'd help 'for free' and ghosted me."** Barter fails.
4. **"I don't know what copy converts. I rewrite it every month and nothing works."** No analytics, no loop.
5. **"My LP works on my laptop but looks terrible on phones."** 80%+ of their traffic is mobile, most ペライチ templates fail at it.

---

## 4. Trigger events (these start the search)

- She lost a high-fit prospect because the prospect googled her and found a stale ペライチ page.
- She's pivoting her niche (e.g., life coach → executive coach) and her old LP no longer matches.
- A peer launched a slick STUDIO site and she's now embarrassed.
- She took a ¥200k 集客 course and step 1 was "fix your LP." Now she needs to fix it.
- She got invited to a podcast/summit and needs a "proper home" to send traffic to.
- A ¥500k launch campaign is coming and her current LP would waste the ad budget.

| **Agency/制作会社** (¥300k–¥1M) | "Overkill and slow." | Rejects on price alone |
| **MOSH / similar coach-niche platforms** | "Locked-in, takes a cut of sales." | Accepts reluctantly — hates revenue share |
| **ZeroEn (our pitch)** | "Too good to be true?" | Needs proof + escape hatch |

## 6. Objections she'll raise to ZeroEn

1. **"Why is it free? What's the real cost?"** → ¥5k/mo is the real cost. Free build means zero upfront risk. We earn our way in by being useful monthly.
2. **"What if I cancel?"** → 6-month commit. After that, cancel anytime. We archive the site, keep the code, you keep your content rights.
3. **"Who owns the code?"** → ZeroEn does. You license the live site through subscription. Like Shopify or Squarespace — you don't own their code either.
4. **"Can you really do it in 3 days?"** → Yes for an LP. Next.js + template + your copy = 48–72h.
5. **"I've been ghosted before."** → Operator name + face + WebMori case study + Stripe (not bank transfer) = accountability.
6. **"Will it look like everyone else's?"** → Custom brand kit per client. Not a template swap.

## 7. What wins her

**The one sentence:** 「前金0円。月¥5,000でLP制作・運用・毎月の改善まで。3日で公開、いつでも微調整。」

**The one story:** WebMori case study — before/after + "3日で公開" + "月¥5,000で運用改善". When she sees a real JP peer using it, objections collapse.

**The one offer structure:** Free build + 6-month commit + Stripe (not Coconala) billing. Coconala is where we meet her, Stripe is where we sign her.

## 9. Copy angles that resonate

### Headline hooks (5)

1. **「あなたの言葉で、クライアントを呼ぶ。LPは無料で作ります。月¥5,000で運用まで。」**
2. 「ペライチで3週末溶かしましたか？ ZeroEnが3日で公開します。」
3. 「前金0円。LP制作と月次改善を月¥5,000で。6ヶ月から。」
4. 「集客の悩みは、LPで8割解決する。作らせてください。無料で。」
5. 「コーチ/セラピストのためのLP。ブランド、スマホ、コピー、全部込みで月¥5,000。」

### Objection reversals (5)

1. **"Free?"** → 「無料なのは制作だけ。運用で月¥5,000いただきます。そのほうがお互い健全です。」
2. **"Cancel?"** → 「6ヶ月だけ一緒に走ってください。効果が出なければ辞めていただいて構いません。」
3. **"Who owns?"** → 「コードはZeroEnが保有します。あなたはサイトをサブスクで使います。Shopifyと同じ構造です。」
4. **"Speed?"** → 「テンプレートじゃありません。あなた専用のブランド設計で3日。WebMoriさんも3日でした。」
5. **"Ghosted before?"** → 「Stripe請求・契約書・運営者実名。毎月レポートが届かなければ返金します。」
