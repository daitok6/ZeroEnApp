## 3. ZeroEn price positioning analysis

### ¥5k Basic — where does it sit?

The buyer's mental comparables at ¥5k/mo are **STUDIO Business (¥3,980–¥5,460/mo)**, **Peraichi Regular/Business (~¥3,940/mo)**, and **Wix JP mid-tier**. All three require the buyer to DIY.

Against those comparables, ZeroEn Basic at ¥5,000/mo delivers **the one thing the SaaS tools cannot**: a custom-built site by a human + AI, ready in 1–3 days, with monthly maintenance. The price is the same or lower than STUDIO Business month-pay (¥5,460), but the deliverable is categorically different.

Against freelance one-shot (¥100k median), ¥5k/mo means **the client breaks even vs. a freelancer at month 20**. For any client who plans to keep the site >2 years, ZeroEn is cheaper — and they never pay upfront.

### ¥10k Premium — what justifies it?

Premium is ~2× Basic but adds: quarterly SEO audit, quarterly security audit (WebMori), 2× included changes or a medium change, full-year analytics dashboard. Each WebMori audit a-la-carte = ¥15,000, so **a single quarterly audit already covers ~3 months of the premium differential**. The math is defensible.

Against agency retainers (¥30k+/mo floor), ¥10k/mo looks aggressive — which is the point. Buyers priced out of agencies find a credible alternative.

### 6-month minimum commitment

**Uncommon in JP SaaS**, common in JP agency work. Peraichi, STUDIO, Wix, Jimdo all allow month-to-month cancellation (though annual pay is incentivised). Agencies and freelancer retainers usually do require 6-month or 12-month contracts.

ZeroEn's 6-month floor positions it as **agency-tier commitment at SaaS-tier price** — consistent with the "free build, we retain code" model. Acceptable for the buyer if ZeroEn leads with the free-build anchor in every sales touch.

### Risks

- **Too cheap (perceived low value):** Real risk. JP buyers equate price with quality. ¥5,000/mo next to a ¥100k Coconala freelancer looks suspicious. Mitigation: lead with the free build (¥0 upfront = ¥100k value anchor), show portfolio proof, and use "限定5社" scarcity.
- **Too expensive for the target:** Minor risk. The target is SMB who rejected Peraichi (too DIY) and rejected Coconala (too expensive upfront). ¥5k/mo sits below their "full-time staff / agency" mental budget.
- **6-month commitment friction:** Real risk on Coconala where buyers expect one-shot. Mitigation: make the commitment the reason the build is free, not a trap.

## 4. Change request pricing in market

**Observed JP freelancer change rates:**
- Small copy edit / section swap: ¥3,000–¥10,000 [^6][^9]
- Medium edit (new section, form, redesign a block): ¥10,000–¥30,000 [^9]
- Full page rebuild / LP redesign: ¥50,000–¥150,000 [^8]

**ZeroEn change catalogue:** ¥4,000 small / ¥10,000 medium / ¥25,000+ large (per `HQ/crm/change-catalogue.md`).

**Competitiveness:** Slightly below market median for small and medium; large is aggressively priced. Directionally correct — ZeroEn changes should be cheaper than ad-hoc freelance because the site is already in the operator's hands (no re-onboarding, no file hand-off). **Keep current pricing; don't raise.**

## 5. Recommendations

### Pricing — keep current model, with two tweaks

1. **Keep ¥5k / ¥10k / 6-month unchanged.** The math works, and the free build is the hook.
2. **Raise Coconala intake from ¥1,000 to ¥3,000 once social proof hits 5+ completed clients.** Rationale: ¥1,000 lets in low-commitment buyers and signals low value. ¥3,000 filters while still being a "buy me a beer" anchor. Test after client #5.
3. **Add a ¥15,000 "Premium+" tier for clients who want one-medium + one-small change per month + quarterly audits.** This captures clients currently upgrading from Premium for ad-hoc requests, and bridges toward the agency-retainer mental model.

### A/B test suggestions

- **Landing page hero copy:** "月¥5,000でプロのLPを" vs "初期費用0円・LPをプロが作ります" — test which anchor converts better.
- **Coconala listing title:** lead with "初期費用0円" vs lead with "月¥5,000〜". Free-build anchor predicted to win.
- **CTA on zeroen.dev:** "相談する" vs "審査を申請する". Application framing should increase perceived selectivity.

### Anchor pricing tactics for Coconala / Lancers copy

1. **Lead with "¥100,000相当のLPを、初期費用¥1,000で"** — directly anchors against the ¥100k Coconala median [^8].
2. **Show the math:** "一般的なLP制作: ¥100,000 + 月額ホスティング ¥3,000 = 1年目 ¥136,000。ZeroEn: ¥1,000 + ¥5,000×12 = ¥61,000" — cut the perceived cost roughly in half against the freelancer+SaaS combination.
3. **Use scarcity: "先着5社無料ビルド枠"** (already live per `HQ/marketing/campaigns/2026-04-first-5/`).
4. **Leverage the 6-month as proof of quality:** "6ヶ月コミットを前提にするからこそ、費用を抑えられます" (the commitment is *why* it's free, not a trap).
