# Dashboard Preview Page — Copy Candidates

## 1. Page Name Candidates

### Option A — "Your Portal"

- **EN eyebrow:** Your Portal
- **EN meta title:** Your Portal — ZeroEn
- **JA eyebrow:** あなたのポータル
- **JA meta title:** あなたのポータル — ZeroEn
- **Positioning rationale:** "Your Portal" shifts the subject from ZeroEn's product to the client's ownership. It signals belonging — this space is already theirs — which reduces friction before the CTA. "Dashboard" sounds internal and tool-like; "portal" implies a destination that belongs to the visitor.

---

### Option B — "Included with Every Plan"

- **EN eyebrow:** Included. Every plan.
- **EN meta title:** What's Included — ZeroEn Client Portal
- **JA eyebrow:** 全プランに含まれる
- **JA meta title:** 全プランに含まれるもの — ZeroEn
- **Positioning rationale:** Directly answers the SMB owner's silent objection ("is this extra?"). Framing the portal as an included benefit — not a upsell — converts skeptics who expect hidden costs from web agencies. The IBM Plex Mono cadence rewards the short, declarative structure.

---

### Option C — "Live from Day One"

- **EN eyebrow:** Live from day one.
- **EN meta title:** Live from Day One — ZeroEn Client Portal
- **JA eyebrow:** 開始日からすぐ使える
- **JA meta title:** 開始日からすぐ使える — ZeroEn
- **Positioning rationale:** "Live from day one" speaks directly to the Japanese SMB owner's fear of being kept waiting or in the dark after signing up. It promises immediacy and transparency, differentiating ZeroEn from agencies that go silent post-contract. Matches the build-in-public tone — confident, concrete, no hedge.

## 2. Pre-CTA Heading Candidates

Current: "What you get when you sign up." — a feature recap that duplicates the hero h1 function.

---

### Option A — "Your site is already waiting."

- **EN heading:** Your site is already waiting.
- **JA heading:** あなたのサイトは、もう始まっています。
- **Why it converts:** Creates mental ownership before the click — the visitor imagines the site already existing, lowering the psychological cost of committing. The past-tense framing ("already waiting") makes inaction feel like the unusual choice.

---

### Option B — "Ready when you are."

- **EN heading:** Ready when you are.
- **JA heading:** あなたが決めたら、すぐ動き出す。
- **Why it converts:** Removes the last micro-friction — reassures the SMB owner that there is no complex onboarding waiting on the other side of the button, only momentum. Short and punchy enough to land immediately beneath the feature tour without competing with it.

---

### Option C — "Five clients. Six months. No risk."

- **EN heading:** Five clients. Six months. No risk.
- **JA heading:** 5社限定・6ヶ月コミット・リスクゼロ。
- **Why it converts:** Stacks three decision triggers in a single breath — scarcity (five clients), clarity (six months), and safety (no risk) — turning the CTA block into a closing argument rather than a feature restatement. Works best if the launch cohort is genuinely limited.
