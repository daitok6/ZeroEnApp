# ZeroEn Marketing Copy Review
**Date:** 2026-04-23  
**Scope:** Full site post-repositioning — homepage, pricing, how it works, startups LP, about  
**Verdict:** Repositioning is structurally solid. The core message is correct. Execution has five specific gaps that will cost scoping calls. Fix the hero H1, surface credentials earlier, add the missing FAQ, sharpen the JA badge copy, and stop hiding the agency comparison behind a scroll.

---

## 1. Hero Clarity — WEAK, needs rewrite

**Current H1:**
> "Production-grade bilingual web products for funded founders and serious businesses in Tokyo."

**Problem:** This is a description, not a hook. A funded Tokyo founder landing here after seeing a cold email or a tweet reads this and thinks "okay, I see what you do" — then bounces without booking. It explains the service but creates no urgency and triggers no recognition of their specific situation.

The subheadline ("Fixed price. No equity. Shipped in weeks.") is actually stronger than the H1. The H1 and subheadline are in the wrong order.

**Suggested rewrite:**

H1:
> "Fixed price. No equity. Bilingual from day one."

Subheadline:
> "Production-grade Next.js + Supabase + Stripe for funded founders in Tokyo — shipped in weeks, not months."

Rationale: Lead with the three differentiators that address the exact objections a funded founder has (will this cost me equity? will the price balloon? is bilingual an afterthought?). Save the description for the subheadline.

**Eyebrow:** "Bilingual SaaS, shipped." is fine as an eyebrow. Keep it.

**Urgency note:** "2–3 active projects at once. Check availability." — this is good. Keep it exactly where it is.

---

## 2. ICP Specificity — GOOD with one gap

The three ICP pillars (funded founders / companies entering Japan / JP startups going global) are specific and correct. The funded founder pillar copy ("You just closed a pre-seed or seed round. You need a bilingual product online before your runway narrows.") is the strongest copy on the entire site. Do not soften it.

**Gap:** The "Companies entering Japan" pillar is noticeably weaker than the other two. "Your HQ approved the Japan launch budget" is fine, but "someone who understands JP legal requirements" is vague — it sounds like a freelancer pitch. The specific legal requirement (特定商取引法 / tokushoho) is called out in deliverablePillars but not here.

**Suggested fix for companies-entering-Japan pillar:**
> "Your HQ approved the Japan budget. You need a bilingual site, JP invoice format, and tokushoho compliance before the launch date — not a six-month agency engagement."

---

## 3. Pricing Page — MOSTLY GOOD, one critical gap

**Growth positioning:** Growth has the green border, the "Most popular" badge, and visual emphasis. Structure is correct. The comparison table (anchor section) showing ¥880k vs ¥5–10M agency is the most persuasive element on the pricing page — but it is buried below the tier cards. A prospect who looks at ¥880k and hasn't seen the anchor table yet may bounce before they get the context they need.

**Recommendation:** Add a single sentence above the tier cards that pre-anchors the comparison:
> "Japanese agencies quote ¥5–10M for equivalent scope. ZeroEn Growth is ¥880k, delivered in 3–4 weeks."

This is already in the subtitle ("Compare ¥880k to a Japanese agency quote...") but it reads like a footnote. Make it a callout above the cards.

**Growth badge — JA copy problem (see section 5):** The EN badge says "Most popular". The JA badge says「最もご好評いただいています」(14 characters, very formal/humble). At the tiny badge size, this is unreadable on mobile and tonally wrong for a startup-facing product. Fix to「人気No.1」or「おすすめ」.

**Feature lists are too generic.** Growth items include "Everything in Starter", "Up to 10 sections + inner pages", "Contact or inquiry form" — these are technical deliverables, not value signals. A funded founder doesn't think in "sections". 

**Suggested rewrite for Growth items (top 3 bullets):**
- "Full bilingual site — EN/JA routing, locale-aware components, JP-style UX"
- "Blog / news for investor updates or product announcements (MDX)"
- "Inquiry form with JP compliance (tokushoho page, JP invoice ready)"

## 4. Objection Handling — ONE FAQ IS MISSING

Current FAQ covers:
- ✓ What's included in the fixed price
- ✓ Do you take equity or revenue share
- ✓ Who owns the code
- ✓ What does "bilingual from day one" mean
- ✓ How do I get started

**Missing: "Why not just hire a Japanese web agency?"**

This is the primary alternative a funded founder evaluates. The anchor comparison table answers it visually but the FAQ should answer it in plain text for prospects who scroll to FAQ first (common on mobile).

**Suggested addition:**
```
Q: Why not just use a Japanese agency?
A: Japanese agencies are built for large coordinated projects with multiple stakeholders — ¥5–10M budgets and 4–6 month timelines reflect that overhead. If you just closed a round and need a production-grade bilingual product live in weeks, the agency model is the wrong structure. ZeroEn is one engineer, fixed scope, fixed price — optimized for the exact window between funding and launch.
```

**Also missing from FAQ:** "What happens if we want changes after delivery?" — the ¥15,000/hr out-of-scope rate is mentioned in a small section below the tiers but not in the FAQ where objections live. Funded founders will ask.

## 5. Japanese Copy Quality — GOOD with two specific issues

Overall the JA copy is business Japanese, not literal translation. Specific praise:
- 「エクイティ不要、SAFEノート不要、レベニューシェア不要」— correct use of katakana business terms
- 「特定商取引法」used correctly (not transliterated)
- 「納品」used consistently throughout (correct, not「デリバリー」)
- 「固定価格」is the right term (not「定額」)
- The ICP pillar descriptions read naturally, not like translated EN

**Issue 1 — Growth badge:** 「最もご好評いただいています」is over-polite for a startup audience. At badge size it also truncates on mobile. Replace with「おすすめ」or「人気プラン」.

**Issue 2 — howItWorks CTA title:** 「始める準備ができましたか？」is a direct translation of "Ready to get started?" and sounds stiff. A Japanese founder reading this would more naturally expect something like「まず30分、話しましょう。」(Let's talk for 30 minutes first.) — more conversational, less questionnaire.

**Issue 3 — lpApply title:** 「プロジェクトのスコープを確認しましょう。」is functional but cold. Opportunity to make it warmer: 「プロジェクトを一緒に整理しましょう。」

Everything else in JA is solid.

## 6. CTA Copy — PASSABLE but one upgrade available

"Book a scoping call" is consistent across all surfaces. It's clear and direct — fine.

**One upgrade:** The footerCta on the homepage ends with "Book a scoping call / 30-minute call. Fixed-price proposal within 48 hours." The note is the most conversion-relevant line on the page (it removes friction: short, free, fast turnaround). This note should be visible on *every* primary CTA, not just the footer.

Specifically: the hero CTA currently has no sub-note beneath it. Add:
> "30 minutes. Proposal in 48 hours."

(Small gray mono text below the button — same treatment as the footerCta note.)

**About page CTA:** "Let's build something." as the section H2 is fine but bland. The subtext ("Fixed price. No equity. Shipped in weeks. Starts with a 30-minute scoping call.") is doing all the work. Consider making the H2 sharper:
> "Ready to ship?"

## 7. Trust Signals — BURIED, needs one surface earlier

**The problem:** Hitachi and Rakuten credentials only appear on the About page. The homepage has no visible credential signal. A prospect who lands on the homepage, reads the hero, and bounces to a competitor never sees the About page.

**What exists:** The "Built by ZeroEn" case studies section on the homepage shows ZeroEn and WebMori as live products. This is good proof-of-execution but it's not founder credibility.

**Fix — Option A (quick):** Add a single line to the hero section beneath the urgency note:
> "Built by a Hitachi + Rakuten-trained engineer."

This is one sentence, no ego, and immediately answers "who is this person" for a prospect who doesn't navigate to About.

**Fix — Option B (structural):** Add a minimal "Built by" block in the homepage between the ICP pillars and deliverablePillars sections. One avatar, name, two logos (Hitachi / Rakuten), and one sentence. Same block already exists on About — bring it up one level.

Option A is a two-minute copy change. Do it now.

## 8. Missing Angle — THE BIGGEST GAP

**The single most important message that's absent:** ZeroEn has no paying clients yet.

This is not a copy problem, it's a positioning problem that copy is currently papering over. The case studies section says "First client / Slot open" in the placeholder card. A sharp prospect will notice.

Currently the site sidesteps this by leaning on ZeroEn itself and WebMori as proof — "Not mockups. These are production deployments we own and operate." This framing is honest and smart. But the absence of a client testimonial or reference is a real credibility gap for ¥880k purchases.

**Short-term copy fix:** The case studies subtitle currently reads:
> "Not mockups. These are production deployments we own and operate."

This is good but slightly defensive. Reframe slightly:
> "We build for ourselves at the same standard we build for clients. Both run in production."

**Medium-term fix:** Get one client. Even a ¥380k Starter client with a one-line quote ("ZeroEn delivered our bilingual site in 12 days. Fixed price, no surprises.") eliminates the single biggest objection block on the site. Until then, consider adding a "currently accepting 2 new clients" signal with a short waitlist note — scarcity framing that reframes the empty slot as selectivity, not absence.
